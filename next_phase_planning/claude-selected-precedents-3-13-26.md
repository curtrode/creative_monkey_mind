# Selected Precedents & Placement Strategy

**Date:** March 13, 2026
**Context:** Portfolio is capped at 10 pages (excerpt.html is already at 10). Precedent material needs to go in the proposal and cover letter instead.

---

## For the Proposal — Selected References Footer

Add 3–4 citations at the bottom of the one-page proposal. No annotations — just names, titles, and the implicit signal that you know the field. Two lines max.

### Suggested entries

- Montfort, Nick. *Memory Slam* / *Stochastic Texts* (2014). Rule-based combinatory poetry — the direct ancestor of Monkey Mind's stochastic engine.
- Branch, Mirowski, Mathewson et al. "Improbotics: Exploring the Imitation Game using Commercial LLM Chatbots" (2024). Multi-model live improv with actors at Edinburgh Fringe — closest precedent, but models auditioned for one role, never in dialogue with each other.
- Zhang & Eger. "Multi-Agent Poetry Generation" (2024). Research framework using multiple LLM agents in cooperative networks for diverse poetry output — computational research, not literary performance.
- Johnston, Jhave. *ReRites* (2017–2019). Book-length AI-assisted poetry — LLM as collaborator in revision, not as a performing voice.

### How to reference in the proposal

One sentence, e.g.:

> While related experiments exist — multi-model improv performance (Branch et al., 2024), multi-agent poetry generation (Zhang & Eger, 2024), AI-assisted poetics (Johnston, 2017) — none stage the transition between generative paradigms as a dramatic event, and none cast distinct models as voices in intimate dialogue.

## For the Cover Letter — Summer Preparation Sentence

One sentence framing summer reading as residency prep. E.g.:

> In preparation for the residency, I will be engaging with recent work in multi-agent creative systems, LLM-assisted poetics, and computational improvisation, including the Improbotics project (Branch et al., 2024) and Allison Parrish's work on computational poetic practice.

## Additional Precedents to Know (not necessarily cited)

These are worth reading over the summer but don't need to appear in application materials:

- **Allison Parrish** — computational poetics, semantic similarity as literary device
- **Taroko Gorge** (Montfort, 2009) — foundational generative poem, widely remixed
- **Sea and Spar Between** (Montfort & Strickland, 2010) — large-scale combinatory poetry
- **"A Defense of LLM-Assisted Poetics"** (Jacket2) — broader critical framing
- **Strachey, Christopher.** *Love Letters* (1952) — the original computer-generated text
- **Knowles & Tenney.** *A House of Dust* (1967) — early stochastic poetry

---

## Why This Approach

The excerpt portfolio (10 pages) is strongest when it shows the work itself — code, word pools, output. Replacing a page of output with a lit review would weaken the portfolio's impact. Precedent awareness belongs in the prose materials where it costs no space and signals field knowledge without competing with the art.
